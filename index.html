Good morning, Student

Your personalised scholarship command centre — track every step of the journey.

⚡
Start document processing immediately. HEC attestation takes 4–8 weeks, IBCC equivalence 4–6 weeks, APS Germany 6–8 weeks. Begin all three in parallel at least 3 months before any deadline.

Your Roadmap

Phase 1 — Documents

  • Renew passport at NADRA if expiring within 2 years
  • Apply for IBCC equivalence immediately (ibcc.edu.pk)
  • Start HEC degree attestation (hec.gov.pk/attestation)
  • Get police character certificate from local station
  • Collect 30+ passport-size photos (white background)

Phase 2 — Tests & Language

  • Book IELTS / TOEFL (British Council or IDP Pakistan)
  • Germany: enroll in German B1/B2 (Goethe Institut Lahore/Karachi)
  • Korea: begin TOPIK prep — topik.go.kr
  • China (Chinese-medium): start HSK prep hsk.neea.edu.cn
  • Take GRE if applying to DAAD or US-style programs

Phase 3 — Applications

  • Write tailored SOP for each country and university
  • Contact 5–10 professors at target universities
  • Request recommendation letters 2 months in advance
  • Register on all scholarship portals (CSC, NIIED, DAAD)
  • Apply to 5–8 scholarships simultaneously — never rely on one

Phase 4 — Visa & Travel

  • Apply for student visa after receiving acceptance letter
  • Get medical fitness certificate from approved hospital
  • Open Sperrkonto blocked account for Germany (Fintiba)
  • Book accommodation before visa appointment
  • Arrange travel insurance before departure

Embassy Track (Recommended)

  • Download GKS form from studyinkorea.go.kr
  • Prepare all documents (see Extra Documents tab)
  • Submit to Korean Embassy Islamabad: 10 Diplomatic Enclave, +92-51-2279380
  • Appear for interview if shortlisted at Embassy
  • Embassy nominates shortlisted candidates to NIIED
  • NIIED matches you with a Korean university
  • Receive acceptance letter → apply for D-2 visa

University Track (More Flexibility)

  • Visit studyinkorea.go.kr → select GKS University Track
  • Choose up to 3 Korean universities from eligible list
  • Submit GKS application directly to each university
  • Each university conducts its own selection process
  • Accepted students go through NIIED for scholarship award
  • More seats and less competition than Embassy Track
💡
Emailing professors before applying dramatically increases your chances — especially for PhD and research-based MS. A professor's recommendation is decisive in GKS University Track.

Where to Find Korean Professors

  • RISS — Korea's academic database: riss.kr
  • Korea Science portal: koreascience.kr
  • Google Scholar: search "[topic] site:ac.kr"
  • University faculty pages: KAIST, SNU, POSTECH, Yonsei
  • ResearchGate and Academia.edu profiles

Key Tips

  • Read the professor's 2–3 latest papers before writing
  • Mention a specific aspect of their research
  • Email format: firstname@[university].ac.kr
  • Follow up once after 2 weeks if no reply
  • TOPIK Level 2+ gives huge advantage for GKS

Track A: Embassy Track (Type A)

  • Register at apply.csc.edu.cn with Agency Code CN92004
  • Fill application form — choose up to 3 universities
  • Print completed form, attach all documents
  • Submit to Chinese Embassy Islamabad: Diplomatic Enclave, St. 5
  • Embassy reviews and forwards to CSC Beijing
  • Results announced May–June. JW202 form issued on acceptance

Track B: University Track (Recommended)

  • Register at apply.csc.edu.cn → select University Track
  • Apply directly through university's international portal (CUCAS or own)
  • Get supervisor's acceptance letter for PhD applications
  • If accepted, university nominates you for CSC scholarship
  • More flexible — no Embassy involvement, year-round intake
  • Having a professor contact dramatically increases success rate
💡
For CSC University Track, a supervisor acceptance letter is often decisive. Many Chinese universities require it before accepting your scholarship nomination.

Pre-Enrollment via Universitaly (Mandatory)

  • Register at universitaly.it — create account
  • Upload qualifications for pre-enrollment (Oct–Dec for next year)
  • University reviews and sends confirmation
  • Use pre-enrollment confirmation for student visa
  • After arriving: formally enroll at university (iscrizione)

Declaration of Value (DoV) — Critical

  • Apply at Italian Embassy Islamabad with all attested academic docs
  • Embassy assesses qualifications and issues DoV
  • Fee: ~€50–100. Takes 2–6 weeks
  • Without DoV, Italian university cannot recognise your degree
  • This step is unique to Italy — most other countries don't require it

DSU Application (After Enrollment)

  • Register with regional DSU office (EDISU, ERSU, DiSCo)
  • Submit ISEE/ISEEU income declaration (family income in EUR)
  • Income below ~€26,000: eligible for grant + accommodation
  • Results announced Oct–Nov. Paid December and April
  • This covers most Pakistani student families if documented correctly

Step 1: APS Certificate (Start FIRST)

  • Apply at aps.org.pk or German Embassy Islamabad APS office
  • Submit all HEC-attested docs + CNIC + form + fee ~PKR 15,000
  • Interview may be required — prepare to discuss thesis in detail
  • APS issued: valid for life. Takes 6–8 weeks.
  • Without APS, German universities CANNOT process your application

Step 2: Apply via uni-assist

  • Register at uni-assist.de — upload docs including APS certificate
  • uni-assist processes and forwards to university
  • Fee: €75 for first application, €30 each additional
  • Some unis have own portals: TU Berlin, RWTH Aachen, TUM, KIT

Step 3: Apply for DAAD

  • Register at daad.de — create applicant account
  • Select scholarship matching your level and field
  • Upload: CV, motivation letter, research proposal, recommendation letters
  • Most deadlines: October–November for following year
  • Selection: committee review + sometimes interview
💡
For DAAD and direct PhD applications, a supervisor letter of intent from a German professor is extremely valuable — sometimes mandatory. Email 15–20 professors; expect 2–5 replies.

Writing Guide

SOP & Application Strategy

Full consultant-level advice to write winning applications from Pakistan.

SOP Structure (Paragraph Breakdown)

  • P1 — The Hook: A specific moment or problem that sparked your interest. Never "since childhood…"
  • P2 — Academic Journey: Key courses, CGPA, relevant academic achievements
  • P3 — Research / Work: Thesis topic, methods, results. Internships, publications
  • P4 — Why Here: Name specific professor + specific paper + why this country
  • P5 — Future Impact: Career plan and Pakistan contribution post-scholarship

Never Write These Phrases

  • "Since childhood I always dreamed of studying abroad…"
  • "Your prestigious university is world-renowned for excellence…"
  • "I am a hardworking, dedicated and passionate student…"
  • Generic family background stories unrelated to research
  • Listing every subject without connecting them to your goals

Recommendation Letter Tips

  • Ask 2 months in advance — professors are very busy
  • Provide: your CV, draft SOP, transcript, scholarship requirements
  • Ask them to highlight: research ability, problem-solving, peer comparison
  • Send a polite reminder 2 weeks before deadline
  • Thank referees regardless of outcome

Pakistan-Specific Strategy

  • Apply to 5–10 scholarships simultaneously — never rely on one
  • Join Facebook groups: "Pakistanis in Korea / Germany / China"
  • HEC also offers its own Overseas Scholarships — monitor hec.gov.pk
  • Make a master spreadsheet: scholarship, deadline, status, portal link
  • Get 5 extra sets of attested documents — use across multiple applications
